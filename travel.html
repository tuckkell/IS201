Destination 01

Hawaii

Hawaii is a paradise unlike anywhere else on Earth. Its chain of volcanic islands offers everything from world-class surfing and snorkeling to lush rainforest hikes and rich Polynesian culture. Here are my top Hawaiian islands ranked, along with the best things to do on each:

  1. Maui — "The Valley Isle"

    Best things to do on Maui:
    • Drive the Road to Hana — a 64-mile scenic highway with waterfalls, bamboo forests, and sea cliffs
    • Watch the sunrise from Haleakala Crater (10,023 ft above sea level)
    • Snorkel or scuba dive at Molokini Crater, a partially submerged volcanic caldera
    • Relax on Kaanapali Beach, consistently rated one of the best beaches in the US
    • Whale watching — Maui is prime habitat for humpback whales December through April

  2. Oahu — "The Gathering Place"

    Best things to do on Oahu:
    • Visit Pearl Harbor and the USS Arizona Memorial — a powerful piece of American history
    • Hike Diamond Head Crater for sweeping panoramic views of Honolulu and Waikiki
    • Surf or bodyboard the famous waves at the North Shore's Banzai Pipeline
    • Explore Waikiki Beach and the bustling nightlife of Honolulu
    • Hike the Koko Head Crater Trail — 1,048 steps straight up for incredible views

  3. Kauai — "The Garden Isle"

    Best things to do on Kauai:
    • Trek the Na Pali Coast via the Kalalau Trail — one of the most dramatic coastlines on earth
    • See Waimea Canyon, often called the "Grand Canyon of the Pacific"
    • Take a helicopter tour over waterfalls and emerald valleys
    • Kayak the Wailua River to Secret Falls
    • Snorkel at Poipu Beach and spot Hawaiian monk seals

  4. Big Island (Hawaii Island) — "The Orchid Isle"

    Best things to do on the Big Island:
    • Visit Hawaii Volcanoes National Park — see active lava flows and ancient craters
    • Stargaze from Mauna Kea Observatory (13,796 ft) on one of the darkest skies in the world
    • Snorkel with manta rays at night off the Kona Coast
    • Visit the black sand beach at Punaluu and look for sea turtles
    • Explore the Waipio Valley, sacred to Native Hawaiians

Destination 02

Italy

Italy is a country that feeds all five senses — jaw-dropping art and architecture, unforgettable cuisine, ancient ruins, and a culture built on passion and beauty. Every city has a story. Here are my must-see Italian destinations:

  1. Rome — The Eternal City

    Essential Rome experiences:
    • Tour the Colosseum and Roman Forum — 2,000 years of history in one afternoon
    • Visit the Vatican Museums and Sistine Chapel (book tickets in advance!)
    • Toss a coin in the Trevi Fountain for good luck
    • Climb the Spanish Steps at sunset for incredible city views
    • Eat cacio e pepe pasta in Trastevere — do not skip this
    • Wander through Piazza Navona and admire Bernini's Fountain of the Four Rivers

  2. Florence — Cradle of the Renaissance

    Florence must-dos:
    • See Michelangelo's David at the Galleria dell'Accademia
    • Climb the Duomo (Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore) for rooftop views
    • Browse the Uffizi Gallery — home to Botticelli's Birth of Venus
    • Cross the Ponte Vecchio at golden hour
    • Take a day trip to Tuscany's Chianti wine country

  3. Amalfi Coast — Where Mountains Meet the Sea

    Amalfi Coast highlights:
    • Drive (or boat) the scenic coastal road between Positano, Amalfi, and Ravello
    • Hike the Path of the Gods (Sentiero degli Dei) with spectacular cliff-top views
    • Swim in the crystal-clear waters of Li Galli islands
    • Eat fresh-caught seafood with limoncello on a terrace overlooking the sea
    • Explore the ruins of Pompeii and Herculaneum nearby

  4. Venice — The Floating City

    Venice experiences:
    • Take a gondola ride through the narrow canals at night
    • Visit St. Mark's Basilica and the Doge's Palace
    • Watch glassblowing on the island of Murano
    • Get lost in the labyrinthine streets (without GPS — trust the adventure)
    • Visit the colorful fishing village of Burano for the best Instagram shots in Italy

Destination 03

US National Parks

Living in Utah means some of the world's most spectacular national parks are practically in my backyard. The American West's canyon country, red rock formations, and high desert landscapes are endlessly awe-inspiring. Here are my favorites:

  1. Zion National Park, Utah

    Top Zion adventures:
    • Hike Angels Landing — a nerve-wracking but rewarding chain-assisted ascent with 360° canyon views
    • Wade through The Narrows — the Virgin River carved a slot canyon you hike through knee-deep water
    • Explore the Emerald Pools trail system for waterfalls and hanging gardens
    • Canyoneer Zion's backcountry slot canyons like Subway and Mystery Canyon
    • Spot California Condors soaring on thermal updrafts above the canyon walls

  2. Bryce Canyon National Park, Utah

    Bryce Canyon highlights:
    • Hike among the hoodoos on the Navajo Loop and Queen's Garden Trail
    • Stargaze — Bryce Canyon has one of the darkest skies in the US (Gold-Tier Dark Sky designation)
    • Snowshoe through the hoodoos in winter for a surreal, otherworldly experience
    • View sunrise from Sunrise Point and watch the orange spires ignite with color

  3. Arches National Park, Utah

    Arches must-sees:
    • Walk under Delicate Arch at sunset — Utah's most iconic image
    • Explore Fiery Furnace with a ranger-guided tour (permit required)
    • Photograph Landscape Arch — one of the longest natural arches in the world at 290 ft
    • Hike the Devils Garden Trail for multiple arches in one route

  4. Grand Canyon National Park, Arizona

    Grand Canyon bucket list:
    • Hike the Bright Angel Trail from the South Rim to the Colorado River
    • Raft the Colorado River through the canyon (multi-day epic adventure)
    • Watch sunrise from Mather Point as the canyon fills with golden light
    • Take a helicopter or small plane tour for the full scale of the canyon
    • Visit the less-crowded North Rim for solitude and stunning vistas
